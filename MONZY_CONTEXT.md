## What Monzy Is

Monzy is an AI-native Accounts Receivable (AR) platform for Indian B2B companies. It deploys AI agents that automate the entire AR lifecycle — from credit decisions upfront, to collections follow-ups, to reconciling incoming payments — so finance teams stop doing manual work and start collecting faster.

**Website:** monzyai.com
**Stage:** Pre-launch / Early Access
**Market:** Indian B2B mid-market (₹20Cr–₹500Cr revenue)

## The Problem We're Solving

### The surface problem
As Indian B2B companies scale, the number of customers with outstanding invoices grows. Following up on overdue invoices, matching incoming payments to the right invoices, and reconciling deductions becomes a full-time manual job — one that's error-prone, slow, and doesn't scale.

The result: cash flow is delayed, DSO (Days Sales Outstanding) keeps climbing, and the AR team is buried in spreadsheets and WhatsApp messages instead of doing anything strategic.

### Why it's worse in India specifically
India has a set of payment complexities that no global AR tool was built to handle:

**1. TDS Deductions**
Customers legally deduct Tax Deducted at Source (TDS) before paying. So if you invoice ₹1,00,000, the customer pays ₹90,000 and files the TDS with the government. The amount never matches the invoice. AR teams spend hours manually reconciling these mismatches every month.

**2. Post-Dated Cheques (PDCs)**
Many Indian businesses — especially mid-market — still pay via post-dated cheques. Companies receive dozens or hundreds of PDCs and track them in Excel. There are no alerts when one bounces, no visibility into what's clearing when.

**3. Payment mode fragmentation**
Indian payments arrive via UPI, NEFT, RTGS, IMPS, cheques, and demand drafts — often with vague or missing references. Matching these to open invoices requires manual detective work.

**4. Remittance advice via WhatsApp**
Customers often send payment confirmations and remittance details over WhatsApp, not email. Global AR tools don't handle this at all.

**5. GST complexity**
Invoicing, e-invoicing mandates, and GST reconciliation add compliance overhead on top of collections work.

**6. Payment culture**
"Payment on pressure" is real in Indian B2B. Companies don't pay until followed up with. Late payment (60–120 day DSO) is culturally normalised. The government even created the MSME Samadhaan portal as a grievance mechanism for delayed B2B payments.

### The full AR lifecycle that's broken

| Stage | What happens manually today |
|-------|----------------------------|
| **Credit** | Sales extends terms based on gut feel. No visibility on buyer risk. Bad debt discovered too late. |
| **Invoicing** | Invoices raised, sent over email or WhatsApp. No tracking of whether they were received or acknowledged. |
| **Collections** | AR team sends follow-up emails manually. Same message copy-pasted 4 times. Some customers never respond. Escalations happen ad-hoc. |
| **Cash Application** | Payment lands in bank. No remittance advice. Someone spends 2 hours matching it to the right invoice across UPI, NEFT, and cheque transactions. TDS deduction creates a mismatch. Manual journal entry to fix it. |
| **Reconciliation** | End of month, someone reconciles everything. Errors from the month compound. CFO asks why DSO is still 90 days. |

## What Monzy Does

Three AI agents that handle the full lifecycle:

### 1. Collections Agent
- Ranks every overdue invoice by risk score and customer payment history
- Runs personalised follow-up sequences automatically (email + WhatsApp)
- Adjusts cadence based on who responds and who doesn't
- Escalates to a human only when it needs judgment
- Outcome: **40% faster collections, AR team freed from manual follow-ups**

### 2. Cash Application Agent
- Matches every incoming payment to the correct open invoice(s) instantly
- Handles UPI, NEFT, RTGS, cheques, and PDCs
- Reads remittance advice from email and WhatsApp
- Accounts for TDS deductions automatically — no manual reconciliation
- Posts matched payments to the ERP
- Outcome: **Zero manual matching, books always accurate**

### 3. Credit Agent
- Evaluates customer creditworthiness before terms are extended
- Sets credit limits and flags high-risk buyers
- Updates limits dynamically as payment behaviour changes
- Syncs with ERP automatically
- Outcome: **Bad debt prevented before it happens**

## Target Customer

### Primary ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

**Company profile:**
- Indian B2B company, ₹20Cr–₹500Cr annual revenue
- 50–500 employees
- Has a dedicated finance team (not just an outsourced CA)
- Uses a real accounting/ERP tool: Tally, Zoho Books, SAP B1, QuickBooks
- Has already bought at least one SaaS finance tool (expense management, payroll, etc.)

**Sectors (in priority order):**
1. **D2C brands selling to modern trade** — Selling to Reliance, DMart, BigBasket means high invoice volume, buyer deductions, and rigid payment terms. Finance teams are young and tech-forward.
2. **B2B SaaS / Tech companies** — Subscription billing, failed payments, TDS on software. Digitally native, fast buying decisions.
3. **Staffing / HR services** — High-volume monthly invoicing, TDS deductions on every payment (194C/194J), clients routinely pay late.
4. **Logistics / 3PL** — One shipment = one invoice. Massive volumes, deductions for delays/damages, POD matching complexity.

**Best entry point within a company:**
- CFO or VP Finance
- Head of AR / AR Manager
- Recently funded companies (Series A/B) — just scaled customer base fast, finance ops hasn't caught up, new CFO is trying to professionalise

**The "modern customer" filter:**
- CFO is reachable on LinkedIn
- Company uses SaaS tools (not just Excel and a CA)
- Has felt the pain personally and knows it's a software problem, not a headcount problem

## Competitive Landscape

### Global players (not built for India)
- **HighRadius** — Dominates Fortune 500, way too expensive and complex for Indian mid-market
- **Growfin** — Indian-founded but positioning globally, doesn't handle TDS/PDC natively
- **Tesorio, Versapay, Kolleno, Upflow** — US/Europe focused, no India-specific workflows

### Indian tools (not modern)
- **Tally** — ERP, basic AR, no intelligence or automation
- **Zoho Books** — Basic dunning, no AI, no TDS reconciliation
- **CA firms** — Manual, not scalable, error-prone

### The gap
No Indian-native, AI-first AR platform that handles the full lifecycle including TDS reconciliation, PDC tracking, UPI/NEFT matching, and WhatsApp-based collections. That's the white space Monzy is built for.
